A painting is mounted on a wall. The bottom of the painting is A feet above eye level, and the top of the painting is B feet above eye level. If you stand directly underneath the painting, you cannot see it at all. Similarly, if you stand very far away, you cannot see it very well. At some distance away from the wall, the angle subtended by the painting, whose vertex is your eye, is maximized.

How far away must you stand from the wall to achieve this maximal viewing angle?

Math.sqrt(A*B)

The angle in question is \theta = \arctan(\dfrac{B}{x}) - \arctan(\dfrac{A}{x}).

\dfrac{d\theta}{dx} = \dfrac{1}{1+(\dfrac{B}{x})^2}\cdot \dfrac{-(B)}{x^2} + \dfrac{1}{1+(\dfrac{A}{x})^2}\cdot \dfrac{(A)}{x^2}

\dfrac{d\theta}{dx} = \dfrac{1}{1+(\dfrac{B}{x})^2}\cdot \dfrac{-(B)}{x^2} + \dfrac{1}{1+(\dfrac{A}{x})^2}\cdot \dfrac{(A)}{x^2}

\dfrac{d\theta}{dx} = \dfrac{A}{A^2+x^2} - \dfrac{B}{B^2+x^2}

Setting this equal to 0, we have \dfrac{A}{A^2+x^2} = \dfrac{B}{B^2+x^2}

Setting this equal to 0, we have A\cdotB^2 + Ax^2 = B\cdotA^2+Bx^2

(A- B)x^2 = B\cdotA^2 - A\cdotB^2

(A- B)x^2 = A \cdot B \cdot (A-B)

x^2 = A \cdot B

x = \sqrt{A*B}

As you can check, the derivative goes from positive to negative as you pass through this critical point. As this is the only critical point, it must be the global maximum. Thus the angle is maximized when x = \sqrt{A*B}.
