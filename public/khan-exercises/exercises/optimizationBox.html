You have a sheet of paper which is W inches wide, and L inches long. You want to make a box by cutting a square out of each corner, so that you can fold up each of the four "flaps" you create. The resulting box has only 5 sides - the "top" is open.

How many inches should the squares you cut out of the corners measure on one side in order to maximize the volume of the resulting box?

(4*W+4*L - sqrt((4*W+4*L)*(4*W+4*L)-48*W*L))/24

The volume of the box is V(x) = x(W-2x)(L-2x), which expands out to V(x) = W*Lx-2*W+2*Lx^2+4x^3. The domain of this function is [0,\dfrac{W}{2}], because x is a length, and so must be positive, and cannot be greater than \dfrac{W}{2} because the sheet of paper is only W inches wide.

Differentiating we have V'(x) = W*L-4*W+4*Lx+12x^2

By the quadratic formula, V' has two zeros, at \dfrac{4*W+4*L \pm \sqrt{4*W+4*L^2-4\cdot12\cdotW*L}}{2\cdot 12}

So either x= \dfrac{4*W+4*L + \sqrt{(4*W+4*L)*(4*W+4*L)-48*W*L}}{24} or x= \dfrac{4*W+4*L - \sqrt{(4*W+4*L)*(4*W+4*L)-48*W*L}}{24}

By inspection, only x= \dfrac{4*W+4*L - \sqrt{(4*W+4*L)*(4*W+4*L)-48*W*L}}{24} falls within the domain of this function.

Since V' is a quadratic with a positive lead coefficient, which has two roots, we know the behavior of its graph: it must go from positive to negative, and back to positive again as it passes through its two roots. The root in the domain is the first one, so the derivative is passing from positive to negative there. Thus this really is a local maximum value. As it is the only local maximum on this interval, it must be the location to the global maximum.

Thus to maximize the are of the box, you should cut out a square with a side length of \dfrac{4*W+4*L - \sqrt{(4*W+4*L)*(4*W+4*L)-48*W*L}}{24}
