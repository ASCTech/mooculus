The derivative of a function tells you the factor by which a small change in inputs scales to become a small change in outputs.

In this case, if we were to wiggle our input by h (from X to X+h), first f would scale that wiggle by f'(X). So now the input of g is wiggling by f'(X)\cdot h (from f(X) to f(X) + f'(X)\cdot h) so g scales that new wiggle by g'(f(X)). In total the wiggle h became f'(X) \cdot g'(f(X)) \cdot h, and so the derivative of g \circ f at x = X is f'(X) \cdot g'(f(X)).

This is the chain rule! To summarize, the chain rule says notation = f'(X) \cdot g'(f(X))

f'(X) = derivfTable[X-1] and g'(fxTable[X-1]) = derivgTable[fxTable[X-1]-1]
