Fred the spherical cow is happily grazing on cubical grass pellets. He grows in volume at a rate of Rate cubic feet per day.

When Fred's radius is Radius feet, at what rate is his surface area growing?

2*Rate/Radius

Let A be Fred's surface area, V be his volume and r be his radius.

Picture

Known Rate: \left. \dfrac{dV}{dt}\right|_{r = Radius} = Rate

Unknown Rate: \left.\dfrac{dA}{dt}\right|_{r = Radius}

Introduction: I will first find a formula for V and A in terms of r. I will use implicit differentiation and the equation relating V to r to find the rate that r is changing at the instant that r = Radius. Then, using the rate information about r, and the equation relating A to r, I will find the rate that A is changing at this instant.

The volume of a sphere is \dfrac{4}{3}\pi times the cube of the radius. So V = \dfrac{4\pi}{3} r^3.
The surface area of a sphere is 4\pi times the square of the radius. So A = 4\pi r^2

Differentiating with respect to time, we obtain \dfrac{dV}{dt} = \dfrac{d}{dt}\left( \dfrac{4\pi}{3} r^3\right)

\dfrac{dV}{dt} = \dfrac{d}{dr}\left( \dfrac{4\pi}{3} r^3\right) \cdot \dfrac{dr}{dt}, by the chain rule.

\dfrac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi r^2\cdot \dfrac{dr}{dt}

At the time of interest, r = Radius and \left. \dfrac{dV}{dt}\right|_{r = Radius} = Rate.

Thus Rate= 4 \cdot Radius^2\pi \cdot \left.\dfrac{dr}{dt}\right|_{r = Radius}

Therefore \left.\dfrac{dr}{dt}\right|_{r = Radius} = \dfrac{Rate}{ 4*Radius*Radius\pi}

Now we can use this in combination with the equation relating radius and surface area to find the rate of change in surface area with respect to time.

A = 4\pi r^2

\dfrac{dA}{dt} = \dfrac{d}{dt} 4\pi r^2

\dfrac{dA}{dt} = \dfrac{d}{dr} \left(4\pi r^2\right) \dfrac{dr}{dt}

\dfrac{dA}{dt} = 8 \pi r \dfrac{dr}{dt}

At the time in question, \left.\dfrac{dr}{dt}\right|_{r = Radius} = \dfrac{Rate}{ 4*Radius*Radius\pi} and r = Radius

So \dfrac{dA}{dt} = 8 \cdot Radius \cdot \dfrac{Rate}{ 4*Radius*Radius\pi} \cdot \pi

Therefore, at the instant that Fred's radius is Radius, his surface area is increasing at a rate of \dfrac{toFraction(2*Rate/Radius)[0]}{toFraction(2*Rate/Radius)[1]}
