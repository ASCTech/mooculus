Which of the limits is equal to f'(a)?

\displaystyle\lim_{h \to 0}\dfrac{f(a+A1h) - f(a+B1h)}{A1-B1h}

The derivative should be a limit of slopes of secant lines to the function. Which of these fractions is the slope of a secant line?

In each of the definitions there are two points we should be focusing our attention on, because f is being evaluated at 2 different values. Which of the fractions represents the slope of the line passing through those two points?

\dfrac{f(a+A1h) - f(a+B1h)}{A1-B1h} is the only expression where which appropriately represents a slope: It is the slope between the point (a+A1h, f(a+A1h)) and (a+B1h, f(a+B1h)). These two points are close to (a,f(a)) when h is small, so the slope of the secant lines between these 2 points should be close to f'(a).

The correct answer is \displaystyle\lim_{h \to 0}\dfrac{f(a+A1h) - f(a+B1h)}{A1-B1h}.
