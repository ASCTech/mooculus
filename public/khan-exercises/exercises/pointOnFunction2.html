By definition, f(X) = Y means (X,Y) is on the graph of f.

Coordinates are listed as (abscissa,ordinate).

So, for ( X, Y ) our abscissa is X and our ordinate is Y.

The abscissa tells us which vertical line the point is on and the ordinate tells us which horizontal line the point is on.

Since our abscissa is equal to X, the point must be on the vertical line which goes through X on the horizontal axis.

style({ stroke: BLUE, strokeWidth: 3, //arrows: "->" }, function() { line( [ X, -12 ], [ X, 12 ]); }); graph.movablePoint.toFront();

Since our ordinate is equal to Y, our point must be on the horizontal line which passes through Y on the vertical axis.

style({ stroke: GREEN, strokeWidth: 3, //arrows: "->" }, function() { line( [ -12, Y ], [ 12, Y ] ); }); graph.movablePoint.toFront();
