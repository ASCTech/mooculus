A light on the ground is A feet away from a building. A B foot tall man is walking from the light to the building at a rate of C feet per second. He is casting a shadow on the side of the building. At what rate is his shadow shrinking when he is D feet from the building?

A*B*C/(A-D)/(A-D)

Let x be the distance between the man and the light source, and let y be the height of his shadow at the same time. Note that when the man is D feet from the building, he is A-D feet away from the light source, so x=A-D at the time we are interested in.

Picture

Known Rate: \dfrac{dx}{dt} = C

Unknown Rate: \left. \dfrac{dy}{dt} \right|_{x = A-D}

Introduction: I will first write an equation relating x and y by using similar triangles. I will the implicitly differentiate this equation with respect to time to obtain an equation relating the rates \dfrac{dx}{dt} and \dfrac{dy}{dt}. I will then use this to find the particular rate of interest, namely \left. \dfrac{dy}{dt} \right|_{x = A-D} .

By similar triangles, \dfrac{x}{B} = \dfrac{A}{y}

Implicitly differentiating with respect to t, we have \dfrac{d}{dt}\dfrac{x}{B} = \dfrac{d}{dt} \dfrac{A}{y}

\dfrac{1}{B} \dfrac{dx}{dt}= \dfrac{d}{dy} \left(\dfrac{A}{y} \right) \cdot \dfrac{dy}{dt}, by the chain rule.

\dfrac{1}{B} \dfrac{dx}{dt}= A\dfrac{d}{dy}( y^{-1}) \cdot \dfrac{dy}{dt}

\dfrac{1}{B} \dfrac{dx}{dt}= -Ay^{-2} \dfrac{dy}{dt}

\dfrac{1}{B} \dfrac{dx}{dt}= \dfrac{-A}{y^2} \dfrac{dy}{dt}

\dfrac{-1}{A \cdot B} y^2\dfrac{dx}{dt}= \dfrac{dy}{dt}

\dfrac{dy}{dt} = \dfrac{-y^2}{A*B}\dfrac{dx}{dt}

This is an equation relating the rates! We know \dfrac{dx}{dt} = C because the man is walking at a constant rate of C feet/second. So we can substitute that into our expression:

\dfrac{dy}{dt} = \dfrac{-y^2}{A*B}\cdot C

This equation tells us, in words, that at any instant, the rate that the shadow is decreasing is the square of the current length of the shadow times \dfrac{C}{A*B}

Thus, to solve the problem, we need to figure out the length of the shadow at the time when the man is D feet from the building, i.e. we need to figure out the value of y when x = A-D. We can do this using the first equation we wrote down relating x and y.

\dfrac{x}{B} = \dfrac{A}{y}

\dfrac{A-D}{B} = \dfrac{A}{y}

\dfrac{A-D}{B} = \dfrac{A}{y}

y \cdot A-D= A \cdot B

y = \dfrac{A*B}{A-D}

Therefore, \dfrac{dy}{dt} = \dfrac{-\left(\dfrac{A*B}{A-D}\right)^2}{A*B}\cdot C when the man is D feet away from the building. Since the question asks for what rate at which the mans shadow is shrinking, this rate is positive.

Thus, the mans shadow is shrinking at a rate of \dfrac{\left(\dfrac{A*B}{A-D}\right)^2}{A*B}\cdot C when the man is D feet away from the building.
