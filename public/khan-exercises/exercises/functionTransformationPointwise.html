If f(x) = y and f(x) = g(x)+c, what point can you determine on the graph of g?

( x ,y-c)

You only have information about a point where x = x.

At this point we have f(x) = g(x)+c.

Substituting f(x) = y we have y = g(x)+c.

Solving for g(x) we have g(x) = y-c.

Thus (x,y-c) is on the graph of g.
