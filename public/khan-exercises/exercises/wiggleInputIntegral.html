Suppose that \displaystyle\int_{L}^{U} f(x) dx = intVAL and f(U) = fVAL.

Approximate \displaystyle\int_{L}^{U+h} f(x) dx as well as you can given this information.

ANSWER

The little bit of extra area we get is approximately the area of the rectangle, or in symbols, \displaystyle\int_{U}^{U+h} f(x)dx \approx h \cdot fVAL = h*fVAL

Thus \displaystyle\int_{L}^{U+h} f(x) dx \approx intVAL + h*fVAL = ANSWER

Note: Rephrasing this, we are really saying that the derivative of the function \displaystyle\int_{L}^{x} f(t)dt at x= U is about f(U). This is the essence of the fundamental theorem of calculus.
